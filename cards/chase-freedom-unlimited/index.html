Chase Freedom Unlimited Review 2026

The Chase Freedom Unlimited is the easiest cash back card recommendation we make at CardTier. It has no annual fee, an easily achievable $200 signup bonus (just $500 in spending over 3 months), 1.5% cash back on everything, and bonus rates on categories most people use every day. There's almost no reason not to have it.

The 1.5% base rate sounds modest compared to the 4X and 5X earning rates on premium cards — but this is genuinely unlimited, applies to every single purchase, and never expires. For purchases that don't hit a bonus category, this beats most competing no-fee cards. And the 3% rate on dining worldwide and drugstores makes it a solid competitor for day-to-day spending.

The first-year value proposition is stronger than the $200 bonus suggests. In year one, Chase gives you a boosted earning structure: 6.5% at grocery stores (up to $12,000 in spend), 4.5% on dining and drugstores, and 3% on all other purchases. This is a legitimate accelerator for new cardholders — someone spending $500/month on groceries in year one would earn an additional $210 in cash back on those purchases alone.

Where the Freedom Unlimited gets really interesting is as part of the Chase ecosystem. If you also hold a Chase Sapphire Preferred or Reserve, your Freedom Unlimited cash back converts to Chase Ultimate Rewards points — at 2.05¢ each when redeemed for travel or transferred to partners. That $200 bonus suddenly becomes $410 in travel value. A 1.5% "cash back" card becomes a 3.075% travel rewards card. This pairing is one of the most popular strategies in the rewards hobby for good reason.

The 0% introductory APR for 15 months is a genuine feature for large purchases. Buying furniture, appliances, or other big-ticket items and spreading payments over 15 months interest-free beats a personal loan at current rates. Trip cancellation insurance, purchase protection, and extended warranty protection round out the benefits for a no-annual-fee card.

CardTier Bottom Line: The Chase Freedom Unlimited is the ideal first or second card for anyone building their wallet. As a standalone card for simplicity seekers, it delivers solid value with zero complexity. Paired with the Sapphire Preferred or Reserve, it becomes a powerful points accumulator that punches far above its weight class.

💡 The Chase Ecosystem Play

Freedom Unlimited + Sapphire Preferred = Powerhouse Combo

When you hold the Freedom Unlimited alongside a Chase Sapphire card, you can convert all your cash back to Ultimate Rewards points and transfer them to airline and hotel partners.

Example: $1,000/month in regular spending earns $15 cash back (1.5%) — OR convert to 1,500 UR points worth $30.75 in Hyatt, United, or other transfers. That's a 2x improvement just by pairing cards in the same ecosystem.
