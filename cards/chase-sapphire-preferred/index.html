Chase Sapphire Preferred Review 2026

The Chase Sapphire Preferred has earned its reputation as the definitive starter travel card — and for good reason. At $95 per year (waived your first year), it punches well above its weight class, delivering rewards that compete with cards costing two to three times as much.

The current 75,000-point signup bonus is worth approximately $1,500 when redeemed through Chase Travel, or potentially more when transferred to airline and hotel partners at a rate of 2.05 cents per point. You'll need to spend $4,000 within your first three months to unlock it — a threshold most people can hit with regular everyday expenses. That bonus alone delivers $1,537.50 in value before you make a single purchase in year two.

Day-to-day earning is genuinely impressive: 3X points on dining worldwide, streaming services, and online grocery purchases, plus 2X on all travel. The included $50 annual hotel credit through Chase Travel effectively brings the annual fee down to $45 for anyone who books even one hotel night per year. Add the DoorDash DashPass benefit (free delivery and reduced service fees through December 2027), and the card easily pays for itself before you count a single reward point.

What truly sets the Sapphire Preferred apart from the competition is access to Chase's Ultimate Rewards transfer ecosystem. Your points move 1:1 to 14 airline and hotel programs, including United MileagePlus, Hyatt, Marriott Bonvoy, Southwest Rapid Rewards, and Air Canada Aeroplan. A 75,000-point transfer to World of Hyatt, for example, could unlock multiple nights at Category 5 hotels that cost $300+ per night at cash rates. This flexibility is rare at this price point.

The card also delivers meaningful travel protections: primary rental car insurance (a big deal — most cards offer only secondary), trip cancellation and interruption insurance up to $10,000 per trip, and baggage delay insurance. These aren't just perks on paper — they're tangible safety nets that can save you hundreds when travel goes wrong.

CardTier Bottom Line: The Chase Sapphire Preferred remains the single best starting point for anyone entering the travel rewards hobby. It has the right combination of accessible annual fee, strong welcome offer, practical everyday rewards, and best-in-class transfer partner flexibility. If you only have one travel card, this should be it.
