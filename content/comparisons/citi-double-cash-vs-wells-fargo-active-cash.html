They're Nearly Identical — Here's What's Different

At the surface level, these two cards are virtually the same product: unlimited 2% cash back, no annual fee, $200 signup bonus. The differences come down to three things:

1. Cell phone protection: Active Cash covers your phone up to $600 (after a $25 deductible) if it's stolen or damaged — as long as you pay your monthly bill with the card. This is worth $10-15/month on its own. Double Cash has nothing comparable.

2. Balance transfers: Citi Double Cash offers a best-in-class 18-month 0% APR on balance transfers. If you're carrying high-interest debt from another card, this is an enormous advantage. Wells Fargo's 15-month intro APR applies to purchases only.

3. Points flexibility: Citi Double Cash earns ThankYou points under the hood. If you also have the Citi Strata Premier or Citi Strata Elite, you can combine your Double Cash rewards and transfer them to airlines like Singapore, Turkish, and Virgin Atlantic — unlocking potentially 5-10x redemption value. Active Cash earns pure cash back with no transfer upside.

Who Should Get Each Card

Get Citi Double Cash if: You're carrying credit card debt and need a balance transfer card, OR you have other Citi ThankYou cards and want to pool points for airline transfers.

Get Wells Fargo Active Cash if: You want the simplest possible 2% card with the best secondary perks ($600 cell protection is a real differentiator), and you don't need balance transfers.

Can't decide? Flip a coin — you genuinely won't go wrong with either. Both are among the top no-fee cash back cards available. Consider which bank you prefer to deal with if all else is equal.
