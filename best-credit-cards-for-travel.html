How to Choose

Flying economy 1-3x/year: Chase Sapphire Preferred. Low fee, strong bonus, 14 transfer partners. Best bang for buck in travel cards.

Flying 5+ times/year, valuing lounge access: Amex Platinum or CSR. The lounge access alone (Centurion, Priority Pass) saves $50-100 per visit.

Want simple travel rewards with no thinking: Capital One Venture X. 2X on everything, credits offset the fee, great lounge access. The simplest premium travel card.

You rent and want a free card: Bilt Mastercard. No-brainer. The only card that pays you for rent. Stack it with any of the above.

💡 The optimal travel wallet for most people: Chase Sapphire Preferred + Bilt Mastercard. Total fees: $95/year. Combined you get CSP's 75K bonus, 14 Chase partners, AND Bilt's rent rewards and unique AA/Emirates partners. Unbeatable at that price.

Understanding Transfer Partners

The secret to getting outsized travel value is transfer partners — moving your credit card points 1:1 to airline or hotel programs that allow you to book premium cabins at a fraction of cash price.

Example: 75K Chase points transferred to Hyatt = potentially 4-5 nights at a $400/night hotel ($1,600-2,000 value). At cash rate, 75K points = $750. Transfer value is 2x better.

Key partners by card: Chase = Hyatt (best hotel program), United, Southwest. Amex = Delta, Hilton, British Airways. Citi = Singapore Airlines (best for international business class). Bilt = American Airlines, Emirates.
