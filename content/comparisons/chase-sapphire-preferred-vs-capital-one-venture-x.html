Chase Sapphire Preferred vs Capital One Venture X 2026

Updated March 2026 · 5 min read · By CardTier

This is the most Googled travel card matchup of the year — and for good reason. Both cards offer 75,000-point welcome bonuses, top-tier transfer partners, and no foreign transaction fees. But they're built for completely different travelers. The Chase Sapphire Preferred targets value-seekers who want maximum point flexibility at a low annual fee. The Capital One Venture X targets lounge lovers who want their premium card to pay for itself. Here's how they stack up.

The Full Breakdown

Welcome Bonus: Near-Tie, Edge to CSP

Both cards launch with 75,000-point/mile bonuses requiring the same $4,000 spend in 3 months. Using CardTier's point valuations (Chase UR @ 2.05¢, Capital One miles @ 1.85¢), the Sapphire Preferred's bonus is worth ~$1,500 versus the Venture X's ~$1,388. That's a $112 difference — meaningful but not decisive. If you're primarily redeeming for domestic flights via Chase Travel (where UR points get a 25% boost), the gap widens slightly in CSP's favor.

Annual Fee: The Central Tradeoff

On paper, the $395 vs $95 fee difference looks enormous — a $300 gap. In practice, it's nearly inverted. The Venture X offers a $300 Capital One Travel credit (easy to use, no category restrictions) plus 10,000 anniversary miles worth roughly $185. That's $485 in guaranteed annual value against a $395 fee — a net gain of ~$90 per year. The Sapphire Preferred offers a $50 hotel credit via Chase Travel, making its effective fee about $45. Both cards are essentially free after credits, but the Venture X edges ahead for heavy travelers who actually use that $300 credit.

Rewards Structure: Flexibility vs Simplicity

The Sapphire Preferred rewards frequent diners and streamers. You earn 3X on restaurants worldwide, 3X on streaming, 3X on online groceries, and 2X on all travel. If your monthly spend skews toward restaurants and Spotify subscriptions, you'll accumulate Chase UR points faster. The Venture X counters with a dead-simple 2X on everything — no categories to track, no activations. Where it really shines: 10X on hotels and rental cars and 5X on flights booked through Capital One Travel. If you book travel frequently through the issuer's portal, the Venture X earns significantly more.

Lounge Access: Venture X Wins Decisively

This is the clearest differentiator. The Capital One Venture X includes access to all three Capital One Lounge locations (DFW, IAD, DEN), plus a full Priority Pass Select membership covering 1,300+ partner lounges worldwide. The Chase Sapphire Preferred has zero lounge access — that's a benefit reserved for the Sapphire Reserve at $795/yr. If you fly more than 4–5 times per year and value pre-flight comfort, the Venture X's lounge access alone is worth $200–$400 annually.

Transfer Partners: Quality vs Quantity

Chase Ultimate Rewards transfers to 14 partners including Hyatt (the gold standard for hotel redemptions), United, Southwest, Marriott, and IHG. Capital One transfers to 15+ partners including Air Canada Aeroplan, British Airways, Virgin Atlantic, and Turkish Airlines — strong international options. The CSP has the edge for domestic travel (especially Hyatt sweet spots). The Venture X has the edge for international business class deals via British Airways Avios or Air Canada Aeroplan.

Travel Protections

Both cards offer trip cancellation insurance, baggage delay insurance, and no foreign transaction fees. The Sapphire Preferred includes a primary rental car collision waiver — a meaningful benefit since many cards only offer secondary coverage. The Venture X includes travel accident insurance and lost luggage insurance. For most trips, both provide comparable protection.

CardTier Net Value Scores

Running both through CardTier's year-1 net value formula (bonus value + annual credits − annual fee):

  • Chase Sapphire Preferred: $1,500 + $50 − $0 (fee waived yr1) = $1,550 net value · A-Tier
  • Capital One Venture X: $1,388 + $485 − $395 = $1,478 net value · A-Tier

In year 2+, the Venture X pulls ahead as the $300 travel credit and anniversary miles continue to offset most of the $395 fee, while the CSP's fee waiver disappears.

The Verdict

Get the Chase Sapphire Preferred if you: Want the best-value starter travel card, love dining out and streaming, plan to maximize Hyatt and United redemptions, or want a strong card without paying $395/year.

Get the Capital One Venture X if you: Travel frequently and value lounge access, want a card that essentially pays for itself every year, prefer simple 2X-on-everything rewards, or need Global Entry/TSA PreCheck covered.

CardTier Bottom Line: The Sapphire Preferred is the better first travel card. The Venture X is the better long-term premium card if you can use the credits. For most people, the CSP wins year 1 — the Venture X wins every year after.
