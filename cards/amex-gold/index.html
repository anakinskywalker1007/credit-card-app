Amex Gold Card Review 2026

The American Express Gold Card is designed for a specific type of spender — one who eats out frequently, cooks at home with quality ingredients, and wants their credit card to actively subsidize both habits. If that sounds like you, this card might be the most profitable piece of plastic in your wallet.

The 100,000-point welcome bonus is one of the most competitive offers in the mid-tier travel card market. At Amex Membership Rewards' valuation of 2.0 cents per point, that's $2,000 in potential value — earned after $5,000 in spending over six months. That spend requirement is higher than average but achievable for most moderate-to-heavy spenders, especially those who use the card for everyday dining and grocery purchases.

Where the Amex Gold truly shines is its 4X earning rate on dining worldwide and at U.S. supermarkets (on up to $25,000 in grocery spend annually). There is no other card at this price point that matches this combination. If you spend $1,500 per month between restaurants and groceries — not unusual for a household — you're earning 72,000 Membership Rewards points per year from those categories alone, worth $1,440.

The annual credits are substantial and significantly offset the $325 fee. The $120 Uber Cash ($10/month, for Uber rides and Uber Eats) and $120 dining credit ($10/month at Grubhub, Cheesecake Factory, Five Guys, and other select merchants) together total $240 — if you use them. A $100 Resy credit ($50 semi-annually) and $84 Dunkin' credit add another $184. Combined, $424 in credits makes the "real" cost of the card closer to negative $99 if you max everything out.

The transfer partner lineup matches Chase's in breadth and arguably exceeds it for international travel. Amex points move to Delta SkyMiles, British Airways Avios, Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer, Air France/KLM Flying Blue, and seven others — including a handful of hotel programs like Marriott Bonvoy and Hilton Honors.

CardTier Bottom Line: The Amex Gold is the best dining and grocery card on the market, period. The $325 annual fee is offset by $424 in credits (if you use them), and the 4X earning rates turn everyday spending into premium travel. The 100K welcome bonus at an elevated offer makes this an easy approval recommendation right now.
