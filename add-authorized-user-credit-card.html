Credit Card Authorized Users

How authorized users build credit

What is an Authorized User?

An authorized user is someone added to your credit card account who can make purchases but is not legally responsible for paying the bill. The primary cardholder is responsible for all charges.

✅ Benefits for Authorized User

Build Credit: The card's history reports to your credit file, helping build credit even if you're new.

No Hard Inquiry: Usually no credit check when added as authorized user.

Access to Rewards: Can earn points/cash back on their spending.

⚠️ Things to Know

Not Responsible: Primary cardholder pays, so it's a privilege not a right.

Can Be Removed: Primary can remove you at any time.

Some Cards Report: Not all issuers report authorized user activity to credit bureaus.

Best Cards to Add Someone

Chase, Amex, and Capital One report authorized user history to credit bureaus. Avoid store cards - many don't report.
