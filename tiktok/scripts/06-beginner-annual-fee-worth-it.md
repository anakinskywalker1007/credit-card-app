# Script 06: Are Annual Fees Worth It? (Beginner Explainer)
**Pillar:** Beginner Explainer
**Target Length:** 40–50 seconds
**Target Audience:** 18–26, skeptical of paying for a credit card

---

## HOOK (0:00–0:02)
**Spoken:** "You should never pay an annual fee for a credit card — UNLESS you know this math."
**On-screen text:** 💳 Annual Fees: Worth It or a Scam?

---

## BODY (0:02–0:42)

**[Beat 1 — Setup the question]**
**Spoken:** "Most people hear '$95 annual fee' and immediately say no. That's actually wrong — and it might be costing you hundreds of dollars in rewards."
**On-screen text:** Saying no to annual fees might actually cost you MORE 🤯

**[Beat 2 — The math]**
**Spoken:** "The Chase Sapphire Preferred is $95 a year. But it gives you: a $50 hotel credit, a $10 monthly dining credit, and 3x on dining and travel. If you eat out twice a month and take one trip a year, you're breaking even in month two."
**On-screen text:** Chase Sapphire Preferred — $95/year
✅ $50 hotel credit
✅ $10/month dining credit  
✅ 3x dining & travel
= Break even in month 2

**[Beat 3 — The rule]**
**Spoken:** "The rule is simple: if the card's credits and rewards exceed the annual fee for how YOU spend — pay it. If they don't — find a no-fee card."
**On-screen text:** Rule: Credits + Rewards > Annual Fee? → Pay it. If not → Free card.

**[Beat 4 — The trap to avoid]**
**Spoken:** "The trap: paying for a premium card and barely using the benefits. That's just donating to Chase."
**On-screen text:** 🚨 Don't pay $550/year and only use the lounge once

---

## CTA (0:42–0:50)
**Spoken:** "CardTier dot com shows you exactly what rewards you'd earn based on how you actually spend. Takes about 30 seconds."
**On-screen text:** ⚡ See YOUR rewards in 30 seconds → CardTier.com
