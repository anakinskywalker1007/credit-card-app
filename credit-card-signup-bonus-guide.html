Credit Card Signup Bonus Guide

How to earn and maximize welcome bonuses

What is a Signup Bonus?

A signup bonus (also called a welcome bonus) is a large reward you get after meeting certain spending requirements within the first few months of opening a card.

Sign-up bonuses typically range from $200 to $1,000+ in value, making them one of the most valuable reasons to get a new card.

How to Maximize Your Bonus

  • Meet the minimum spend early: Don't wait until the last month
  • Use natural spending: Don't overspend just to meet the bonus
  • Set up autopay: Helps ensure you don't miss payments
  • Track your spending: Make sure you're on pace to hit the bonus
  • Don't cancel too early: Wait at least 12 months to avoid clawback

Important Terms to Know

Minimum Spend: The amount you need to spend in a specific time period to earn the bonus (usually $1,000-$8,000).

Spending Period: The timeframe to meet the minimum spend (typically 3-6 months).

Clawback: If you close the card within the first year, the issuer may take back your bonus.

Is a Signup Bonus Worth It?

Yes, if you can naturally meet the minimum spend without overspending. The key is to calculate whether the bonus exceeds the annual fee and if the card's benefits match your spending habits.
